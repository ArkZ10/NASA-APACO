The similarity between human and mouse livers is one of the primary reasons mice are used as test subjects in preclinical liver research. The structural, functional, and genetic similarities allow scientists to study liver diseases, drug metabolism, and potential treatments in mice before moving on to human trials.

Similar Metabolic Functions
Both human and mouse livers perform essential metabolic functions such as detoxification, lipid metabolism, glucose regulation, and protein synthesis. LIVER is central to regulating these processes in both species, making it an ideal organ to study metabolic disorders and drug metabolism using mice..
